THE DIRECTORS

CHRIS SANDERS

Chris Sanders is an American animator, writer, and director, best known for his work on animated films such as "Lilo & Stitch" and "How to Train Your Dragon." He began his career at Walt Disney Feature Animation, where he worked on films like "The Lion King" and "Mulan." Sanders is known for his unique artistic style and his ability to create endearing, relatable characters. He has won numerous awards for his work in the animation industry and continues to be a highly respected figure in the field. After leaving Disney in the early 2000s, Sanders joined DreamWorks Animation, where he wrote and directed "Lilo & Stitch," a critically acclaimed film that was both commercially successful and widely praised for its storytelling and character development. He also served as co-writer and co-director of the popular "How to Train Your Dragon" franchise, which went on to become one of DreamWorks' most successful and beloved franchises.

Sanders is known for his distinctive visual style, which often incorporates elements of traditional animation with modern technology. He is also known for his ability to create complex, fully realized characters that audiences can connect with on an emotional level. In addition to his work in animation, Sanders has also published several children's books and worked on a variety of other creative projects, including video games and television shows. Overall, Sanders is widely regarded as one of the most talented and innovative figures in modern animation.

DEAN DEBLOIS

Dean DeBlois is a Canadian film director, writer, and animator, best known for his work on the "How to Train Your Dragon" franchise. He began his career as an animator at Hinton Animation Studios in Ottawa, Canada, before moving to Los Angeles to work at Don Bluth Studios and later at Walt Disney Feature Animation. While at Disney, DeBlois worked on several successful films, including "Mulan" and "Lilo & Stitch."

In 2008, DeBlois joined DreamWorks Animation as the writer and co-director of the first "How to Train Your Dragon" film, which was a critical and commercial success. He went on to direct and co-write the second and third films in the franchise, cementing his status as one of the key creative forces behind the beloved series. DeBlois is known for his ability to craft rich, emotionally resonant stories, and for his skill at creating dynamic, visually stunning animated sequences.

In addition to his work on the "How to Train Your Dragon" films, DeBlois has also directed and written other animated features, including "Lilo & Stitch 2: Stitch Has a Glitch" and "Mulan II." He has been nominated for numerous awards for his work, including Academy Awards and Golden Globe Awards, and is widely regarded as one of the most talented and influential figures in contemporary animation.
